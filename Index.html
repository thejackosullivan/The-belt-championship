The Origin
How It All Started

It was Sam Johnson who started it. He pulled a camo canvas belt — cannabis buckle and all — from his golf bag. It belonged to Frank Rennie. Sam suggested they play for it. Nobody said no.

That was 2011. Since then, the winner of each year's Belt Championship earns the right to sew a patch onto the belt itself — their initials and the year, stitched into the fabric of the thing. A permanent record of glory, carried into every future competition.

What began as a throwaway suggestion at Boughton Golf Club is now a fully-fledged circuit. Four events, seven players, and one very important piece of camo canvas.
